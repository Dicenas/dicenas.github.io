Bad Little Birdies
Five bad little birdies sat high on a wire,
Each full of mischief, each playing with fire.
Their leader, so daring, with tricks up his sleeve,
Urged on the others, who’d all soon cease to breathe.

The first spied a cat with a flick of its tail,
Decided to prank it - how this fail?
With a leap and a pounce, the cat took its prize,
Now four birdies remained, and none looked too wise.

The leader then sneered, “Go guard the bird bath,
Scare off the others! Go show them your wrath!”
It scared off the flock but returned to the wire,
Where it touched the wrong spot and went up in fire.

Three bad little birdies, their antics not through,
One chased a hummingbird, swift as it flew.
Across the wide road, it zipped with a squawk,
Till it met a fast truck - and now there were two.

The fourth spied a window, its reflection so clear,
Thinking that it saw a rival bird near.
It dove at full speed with a challenging cry,
But the glass didn’t budge, and the bird said goodbye.

The last, their bold leader, spied a feeder so fine,
It slipped through the bars, thinking, “These seeds are all mine!”
But predators circled, their claws scraping near,
The bad birdie's bravado dissolved into fear.

The feeder shook wildly, the seeds spilled below,
The leader watched helplessly, his hunger would grow.
Taunted by beasts who could not break through,
The leader stayed put, unsure what to do.
Trapped in the cage, with no way to eat,
It whimpered and cried, accepting defeat.
With nothing to eat, the leader grew thin,
Trapped with his shame in the cage it died in.
